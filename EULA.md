**Categories of Data Subjects whose Personal Data will be Processed by Vendor:**
- Customer's employees, contractors, and authorised personnel who access or administer the Company Products and/or Services;
- Customer's end users and customers (including registered account holders and visitors) who interact with applications or systems built or operated using the Company Products and/or Services;
- Counterparties and wallet holders whose blockchain addresses appear in transactions initiated by Customer through the Company Products and/or Services;
- Any other Data Subjects whose Personal Data is submitted to the Company Products and/or Services by or on behalf of Customer.

**Categories of Personal Data to be Processed:**
- Identity and contact data: name, email address, username, account ID, organisation, role/title;
- Authentication data: hashed passwords, API keys, OAuth tokens, session tokens, multi-factor authentication factors;
- Technical data: IP address, device identifiers, browser user-agent, operating system, referring URLs, language preference;
- Usage data: feature usage, interaction logs, audit logs, request and response metadata, error and diagnostic logs;
- Transactional data: blockchain wallet addresses, transaction hashes, on-chain interactions, payment metadata (excluding full payment-card numbers);
- Content data: prompts, files, models, scenes, messages, and any other content uploaded to or generated through the Company Products and/or Services by Customer or its Data Subjects;
- Support and communication data: correspondence with Vendor's support team, including any Personal Data voluntarily provided therein.

**Sensitive data to be Processed (if applicable) and any enhanced protections applied to the sensitive data:**
The Company Products and/or Services are not designed or intended to Process special categories of Personal Data (as defined under Article 9 of the EU GDPR), including data revealing racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs, trade union membership, genetic data, biometric data for the purpose of uniquely identifying a natural person, data concerning health, or data concerning a natural person's sex life or sexual orientation. Customer shall not submit such data unless expressly agreed in writing in advance. Where the parties have so agreed, enhanced protections shall include: (i) encryption at rest using AES-256 and in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher; (ii) strict role-based access controls limited to a named list of Authorised Persons; (iii) detailed access logging and quarterly access reviews; and (iv) segregated processing environments.

**The frequency of the transfer (e.g. whether the data is transferred on a one-off or continuous basis):**
Continuous for the duration of the Services Agreement.

**Nature of the Processing:**
Hosting, storage, transmission, computation, indexing, querying, logging, monitoring, backup, restoration, and deletion of Customer Data as required to provide the Company Products and/or Services described in the Services Agreement, including but not limited to: rendering and serving 3D scenes and assets; orchestrating AI and agent workloads; routing and recording on-chain payment instructions; serving paid API endpoints and marketplace integrations; and providing technical support to Customer.

**Purpose(s) of the data Processing:**
Customer Data will be Processed for the purpose of performing the Services described in the Services Agreement.

**The period for which the Personal Data will be retained, or, if that is not possible, the criteria used to determine that period:**
The Vendor will Process Customer Data for the duration of the Services Agreement. Upon termination of the Services Agreement, it will be deleted in accordance with this DPA. Operational logs and backups containing Personal Data shall be retained for no longer than ninety (90) days following termination, after which they shall be securely destroyed, unless retention is required by applicable law (in which case the data shall be isolated and protected from further Processing until deletion is possible).

**For Processing by (sub-) Processors, also specify subject matter, nature and duration of the Processing:**
As described in the Services Agreement and above. Each sub-Processor Processes Customer Data only as necessary to support a specific layer of the Company Products and/or Services (e.g. cloud hosting, AI inference, blockchain RPC, observability, transactional email) and only for the duration of the Services Agreement or, if shorter, the duration of Vendor's engagement of that sub-Processor.

**The countries or territories in which the Personal Data will be processed:**
- Singapore (primary processing region);
- United States (cloud hosting, AI inference, observability);
- European Union (cloud hosting failover, transactional email);
- Such other territories as listed in Appendix 3 from time to time.

# Appendix 2 — Minimum Security Measures

Vendor shall at a minimum ensure the following security measures:

- **Information security policy:** Vendor will implement and adhere to a written information security policy that specifies the security standards it will apply to protect the Personal Data it processes in accordance with this DPA. The information security policy will mandate the use of appropriate technical and organisational security measures in the Vendor's organisation to protect Personal Data against unauthorised and unlawful processing and against accidental loss, damage or destruction. It will further describe the measures to be taken in the event of an actual or suspected data or security breach.
- **Information security officer:** Vendor will appoint a duly skilled employee with responsibility for ensuring the security of Personal Data processed by Vendor in its organisation and for reviewing, maintaining and updating the Vendor's information security policy.
- **Physical security:** Access to data processing facilities will be restricted to duly authorised employees and contractors by use of keys, fingerprint readers, or other electronic security measures.
- **Firewall and anti-virus:** Vendor will implement appropriate firewall, anti-virus, anti-spyware and other anti-malware software and technologies on all networks and systems it uses to process Personal Data. Vendor will update its firewall, anti-virus, anti-spyware and other anti-malware software and technologies on a regular basis to ensure that they protect against then-current virus, spyware and other malware threats.
- **Access controls:** Vendor will implement technical access controls that restrict access to Personal Data it processes to duly authorised employees and contractors only. Duly authorised employees and contractors will be permitted to access Personal Data only to the extent necessary for the performance of their duties. Vendor will identify and appoint a system administrator with overall responsibility for granting, changing or voiding data access privileges to its data processing systems.
- **Usernames / passwords:** Access to Personal Data will be controlled through access privileges (described above), usernames and confidential passwords. No two employees or contractors may share or use the same username. Employees and contractors will be required to change their passwords on a regular basis and at least once every six months. All employee passwords must be at least eight characters, including a minimum of one uppercase letter and one numeral.
- **Back-up:** Vendor will take regular, at least weekly, back-ups of the Personal Data that it processes on behalf of Customer. Data back-ups will be stored securely at an offsite location and will be available for data restoration within a reasonable period of time.
- **Disaster recovery / business continuity:** Vendor will implement and adhere to appropriate disaster recovery and business continuity plans that will ensure the availability, security, integrity and (where necessary) restoration of the Personal Data on the occurrence of a force majeure or similar business interruption event. Vendor will provide a copy of its disaster recovery and business continuity plans to Customer upon request.
- **Power loss:** Vendor's data processing systems will protect against loss, destruction or damage of Personal Data due to failure or interference of any power supply.
- **Audit:** Vendor will audit its compliance with its information security policy on a regular basis. Any remedial measures identified as necessary following an audit will be reviewed and implemented as appropriate under the circumstances.
- **Encryption:** Vendor will encrypt Personal Data in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher and at rest using AES-256 (or equivalent industry-standard cryptography). Encryption keys are managed via a hardware-backed key management service with strict access controls and rotation.
- **Network segmentation:** Production systems Processing Personal Data are logically segmented from development, staging, and corporate networks. Inbound access to production is restricted to a hardened bastion or zero-trust gateway with multi-factor authentication.
- **Multi-factor authentication (MFA):** MFA is enforced for all administrative access to systems Processing Personal Data, for all source-control and CI/CD systems, and for all cloud-provider consoles.
- **Vulnerability management:** Vendor performs continuous dependency scanning, periodic penetration testing (at least annually), and patches critical vulnerabilities within seven (7) days of confirmed disclosure.
- **Secure development lifecycle:** Code changes are peer-reviewed, undergo automated security analysis, and are deployed through reproducible CI/CD pipelines with audit trails.
- **Logging and monitoring:** Security-relevant events (authentication, privilege changes, data access, configuration changes) are centrally logged, retained for a minimum of twelve (12) months, and continuously monitored for anomalies.
- **Personnel screening and training:** Authorised Persons undergo background checks where permitted by law and complete mandatory data-protection and security awareness training annually.

The following table sets out specific technical and organisational measures the Vendor will implement to support the fulfilment of Data Subject rights requests made to Customer:

| Measure | Description |
|---|---|
| Self-serve administrative console | Customer administrators can directly access, export, correct, or delete Personal Data records belonging to their Data Subjects through Vendor's administrative console, without needing to file a support ticket. |
| Data export API | Vendor exposes a documented API endpoint that returns all Personal Data associated with a given Data Subject identifier in a structured, machine-readable format (JSON), supporting the right to data portability. |
| Erasure API | Vendor exposes a documented API endpoint that, on request, performs a verified hard-delete of all Personal Data associated with a given Data Subject identifier across primary stores, search indices, and caches, with deletion from backups completing within the backup-retention window. |
| Audit log | All Data Subject rights actions executed via the console or API are recorded in an immutable audit log accessible to Customer. |
| DSR support workflow | A dedicated support workflow handles Data Subject rights requests routed to Vendor in error, with a documented service-level objective of acknowledging within seventy-two (72) hours and forwarding to Customer. |

# Appendix 4 — Jurisdiction-Specific Terms

This Appendix 4 sets out jurisdiction-specific terms and conditions with which the parties must comply. Capitalised terms used in this Appendix 4 shall have the meanings given to them in the main body of this DPA, except where expressly indicated otherwise.

## Section I — EU/UK Data Protection Terms

**Scope and conflicts:** Where and to the extent that Customer Data Processed by Vendor pursuant to the Services Agreement is subject to EU/UK Data Protection Law, this Appendix 4, Section I shall apply.

**Definitions:** The following terms shall have the following meanings:
- "Controller", "Processor", "Data Subject", "Personal Data" and "Processing" (and "Process") shall have the meanings given in EU/UK Data Protection Law;
- "EEA" means the European Economic Area;
- "EU/UK Data Protection Law" means: (i) Regulation 2016/679 of the European Parliament and of the Council on the protection of natural persons with regard to the Processing of Personal Data and on the free movement of such data (General Data Protection Regulation) (the "EU GDPR"); (ii) the EU GDPR as saved into United Kingdom law by virtue of section 3 of the United Kingdom's European Union (Withdrawal) Act 2018 (the "UK GDPR"); (iii) the EU e-Privacy Directive (Directive 2002/58/EC); and (iv) any and all applicable national data protection laws made under, pursuant to or that apply in conjunction with any of (i), (ii) or (iii); in each case as may be amended or superseded from time to time;
- "Restricted Transfer" means: (i) where the EU GDPR applies, a transfer of Personal Data from the EEA to a country outside of the EEA which is not subject to an adequacy determination by the European Commission; and (ii) where the UK GDPR applies, a transfer of Personal Data from the United Kingdom to any other country which is not subject based on adequacy regulations pursuant to Section 17A of the United Kingdom Data Protection Act 2018; and
- "Standard Contractual Clauses" means: (i) where the EU GDPR applies, the contractual clauses annexed to the European Commission's Implementing Decision 2021/914 of 4 June 2021 on standard contractual clauses for the transfer of Personal Data to third countries pursuant to Regulation (EU) 2016/679 of the European Parliament and of the Council ("EU SCCs"); and (ii) where the UK GDPR applies, standard data protection clauses adopted pursuant to or permitted under Article 46 of the UK GDPR ("UK SCCs").

**Restricted transfers:** The parties agree that when the transfer of Customer Data from Customer to Vendor is a Restricted Transfer it shall be subject to the appropriate Standard Contractual Clauses as follows:

(a) in relation to data that is protected by the EU GDPR, the EU SCCs will apply completed as follows:
1. Module Two will apply (where Customer is a Controller of Customer Data);
2. Module Three will apply (where Customer is a Processor of Customer Data on behalf of a third party Controller);
3. in Clause 7, the optional docking clause will apply;
4. in Clause 9, Option 2 will apply, and the time period for prior notice of sub-Processor changes shall be as set out in Clause 7 of this DPA;
5. in Clause 11, the optional language will not apply;
6. in Clause 17, Option 1 will apply, and the EU SCCs will be governed by the law of The Netherlands;
7. in Clause 18(b), disputes shall be resolved before the courts of The Netherlands;
8. Annex I of the EU SCCs shall be deemed completed with the information set out in Annex I to this Appendix 4; and
9. Annex II of the EU SCCs shall be deemed completed with the information set out in Annex II to this Appendix 4.

(b) in relation to Customer Data that is protected by the UK GDPR, the UK SCCs will apply completed as follows:

(i) For so long as it is lawfully permitted to rely on standard contractual clauses for the transfer of personal data to processors set out in the European Commission's Decision 2010/87/EU of 5 February 2010 ("Prior C2P SCCs") for transfers of personal data from the United Kingdom, the Prior C2P SCCs shall apply between Customer (which, where Customer is a processor it enters for the benefit of the relevant third party controller) and the Vendor on the following basis:
- Appendix 1 shall be completed with the relevant information set out in Annex I to this Appendix 4;
- Appendix 2 shall be completed with the relevant information set out in Annex II to this Appendix 4; and
- the optional illustrative indemnification Clause will not apply.

(ii) Where sub-clause (b)(i) above does not apply, but Customer and the Vendor are lawfully permitted to rely on the EU SCCs for transfers of personal data from the United Kingdom subject to completion of a "UK Addendum to the EU Standard Contractual Clauses" ("UK Addendum") issued by the Information Commissioner's Office under s.119A(1) of the Data Protection Act 2018, then:
- (A) the EU SCCs, completed as set out above in clause 3(a) of this Section I shall also apply to transfers of such Customer Data, subject to sub-clause (B) below;
- (B) the UK Addendum shall be deemed executed between the transferring Customer and the Vendor, and the EU SCCs shall be deemed amended as specified by the UK Addendum in respect of the transfer of such Customer Data.

(iii) If neither sub-clause (b)(i) or sub-clause (b)(ii) applies, then Customer and the Vendor shall cooperate in good faith to implement appropriate safeguards for transfers of such Customer Data as required or permitted by the UK GDPR without undue delay; and

(c) in the event that any provision of this DPA contradicts, directly or indirectly, the Standard Contractual Clauses, the Standard Contractual Clauses shall prevail.

**Onward transfers:** Vendor shall not participate in (nor permit any sub-Processor to participate in) any other Restricted Transfers of Customer Data (whether as an exporter or an importer of Customer Data) unless: (i) it has first obtained Customer's prior written consent; and (ii) the Restricted Transfer is made in full compliance with EU/UK Data Protection Law and pursuant to Standard Contractual Clauses implemented between the exporter and importer of Customer Data.

**Deletion or return of Customer Data:** Clause 9 of this DPA shall not apply to the extent that Vendor is required by any applicable EU or any EU Member State law (for Customer Data that is subject to the EU GDPR) or UK law (for Customer Data that is subject to the UK GDPR) to retain some or all of Customer Data, in which event Vendor shall isolate and protect Customer Data from any further Processing except to the extent required by such law until deletion is possible.

## Section II — Singapore (PDPA)

Where Customer Data is subject to the Singapore Personal Data Protection Act 2012 ("PDPA"):
- Vendor shall Process Personal Data only for purposes that a reasonable person would consider appropriate in the circumstances and as instructed by Customer.

- Vendor shall notify Customer without undue delay of any data breach affecting Customer Data so that Customer can comply with its data breach notification obligations under the PDPA.
- The parties acknowledge that Vendor acts as a data intermediary within the meaning of the PDPA.

## Section III — California (CCPA/CPRA)

Where Customer Data includes "personal information" of California consumers within the meaning of the California Consumer Privacy Act of 2018, as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act ("CCPA/CPRA"):
- Vendor is a "service provider" and shall not (i) sell or share personal information; (ii) retain, use, or disclose personal information for any purpose other than for the business purposes specified in the Services Agreement, including to retain, use, or disclose the personal information for a commercial purpose other than providing the Services; (iii) retain, use, or disclose the personal information outside the direct business relationship between the parties; or (iv) combine the personal information with personal information received from another source, except as permitted by the CCPA/CPRA.
- Vendor certifies that it understands these restrictions and will comply with them.
- Vendor shall provide reasonable assistance to Customer in responding to verifiable consumer requests under the CCPA/CPRA.
